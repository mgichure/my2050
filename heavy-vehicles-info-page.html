Heavy Vehicles (Transport)

EV's reduce CO2 emissions from burning fossil fuels associated with many other forms of travel

This lever is set at AMBITION LEVEL:

2

This lever allows you to decide how our heavy vehicles are powered by setting an ambition level for heavy vehicle technology by 2050, ranging from level 1 (minimum effort) to level 4 (maximum effort).

Heavy vehicles like planes, ships, trains, buses, and HGVs rely on fossil fuels, emitting carbon dioxide. My2050 aims to decarbonize them through electrification. 65% of UK passenger trains are already electric. Switching other heavy vehicles to electricity would eliminate emissions, but larger batteries may reduce cargo capacity. Limited electric vehicle charging infrastructure is a challenge. Hydrogen and hybrid engines are alternative options for decarbonization, with hydrogen producing water when burned. Hybrid engines have a smaller decarbonization impact.

Very little change compared with today, as the proportion of passenger trains that are electric stays at 65%, and the share of electric heavy vehicles (excluding trains) remains at 0%, with most still fuelled by fossil fuels.

The proportion of passenger trains that are electric rises to 80%, and 40% of buses are electric. Only 2% of articulated HGVs are electric, however.

The proportion of passenger trains that are electric rises to 90%, and 60% of buses are electric. The share of articulated HGVs that are electric increases to 15%, and 50% of planes are plug-in hybrid (though this only slightly decarbonises air travel, as it's not thought possible to make large airliners fully electric).

All buses, trains, and articulated HGVs are electrified, and all planes are plug-in hybrid.

Interaction with other levers

For electric vehicles to have the greatest effect on reducing carbon dioxide emissions, they must use low carbon electricity which needs to be generated using the Electricity levers for nuclear and renewables, or the Carbon Capture lever to decarbonise electricity produced from fossil fuels. Aviation would need to switch to biofuels using the Bioenergy lever to fully decarbonise.
